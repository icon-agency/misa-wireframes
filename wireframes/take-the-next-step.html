Get Advice From Experts

You don't have to figure this out alone.

Career advisors, training organisations, and support networks are here to help you take the next step. Whether you want help with planning, applications, or exploring options, you've got people in your corner.

You don't need all the answers. You just need to ask the right questions.

Your school's career adviser or relevant teacher is one of the best people to talk to right now. They can help you figure out your next steps—whether that's choosing subjects, applying for apprenticeships, or finding the right training course. Don't wait until Year 12. If you're even a little interested in manufacturing, now's the time to start the conversation.

Ask your career adviser or relevant teacher

  • "What manufacturing jobs could suit me?"
  • "Are there local employers offering apprenticeships?"
  • "What training courses should I look at?"
  • "Can you help me with my resume or application?"

Explore training options near you

TAFE and other training organisations are a great way to gain practical, job-ready skills. Many manufacturing careers start here.

Even if you're unsure of the exact path, speaking with your career adviser at school or checking out websites for your local training organisation can open doors you didn't know existed.
